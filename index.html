About Me

Hello, my name is Whithedel, but you can also call me Ricky. I was born into a wealthy family in Haiti, where my father was a gynecologist and the spokesman for the president, and my mother ran a successful small business. My early years were filled with joy and innocence, untouched by the complexities of adult life. However, my family faced significant challenges during the political crisis of 2004, which led us to seek political asylum and move to the United States.

Adapting to life in the U.S. was challenging due to the language barrier and frequent relocations. After struggling with high school due to these issues, I earned my GED and pursued higher education at Miami Dade College and Broward College, focusing on computer information systems and computer science. Despite financial difficulties preventing me from completing my degree, I remained determined to succeed.

My professional journey included various jobs, from a stint in a pyramid scheme to roles in auto repair and vehicle parts delivery, before transitioning to a sales consultant role in the automotive industry. Each experience taught me resilience and adaptability, qualities that have been invaluable in my career.

I embarked on a new chapter by enrolling in a coding bootcamp at Columbia University, funded through a personal loan. This decision marked a significant pivot towards technology, leading to my current role as a Collibra Solutions Architect. Since then, I have worked with high-profile clients like T-Mobile, Wells Fargo, and DTCC, applying my skills in data governance, system architecture, and project management to enhance enterprise data systems.

Today, I am proud to be a father and a husband, striving to blend my technical acumen with my personal experiences to create meaningful solutions. Despite the setbacks, my journey from Haiti to the tech industry reflects my resilience and commitment to growth and excellence.

I'm excited about the future and eager to continue making impactful contributions to the field of data governance and technology.
